Tier 01 — Any Citizen — 15 Minutes

Learn. Check. File.

You do not need technical skills. You do not need a lawyer. You need a browser, fifteen minutes, and a willingness to ask your government a question it does not want to answer.

Step 1

Understand What Flock Is

Flock Safety sells automated license plate readers (ALPRs) to police departments. Every camera captures your plate, vehicle description, and timestamp — every time you pass. This data feeds a centralized national database controlled by a private corporation in Atlanta. It is not a local camera network. It is a mass surveillance system with documented ICE access, no warrant requirement, and audit logs that show queries justified by a single word.

Documented

A Texas sheriff queried the Flock database with the justification "had an abortion, search for female" — an actual logged query in the system.

Step 2

Check If Your City Has Flock

DeFlock.me maintains a crowdsourced map of 76,000+ license plate reader locations nationwide, including Flock cameras. Search your city. Check your city council minutes and police department press releases for mentions of "Flock Safety," "ALPR," or "license plate reader." Check budget line items for Flock payments. If your city has them, you have standing to act.

Step 3

File a Public Records Request

Every U.S. state has a public records law (FOIA at the federal level, state equivalents everywhere else). You have a statutory right to request government documents. File the following request with your city clerk, police department, and city administrator. This is the single most important action you can take — it creates a legal paper trail and forces the city to respond on the record.
FOIA / Open Records Request Template
Subject: Public Records Request — Flock Safety / ALPR Deployment To: [CITY CLERK / RECORDS CUSTODIAN] [CITY/TOWN NAME], [STATE] Dear [NAME OR TITLE], Pursuant to [YOUR STATE'S PUBLIC RECORDS STATUTE — e.g., Wisconsin Public Records Law, Wis. Stat. § 19.31-19.39], I am requesting copies of the following records related to any automated license plate reader (ALPR) system operated by or on behalf of [CITY/TOWN NAME], including but not limited to systems provided by Flock Safety, Inc.: 1. The complete contract or agreement between [CITY/TOWN NAME] and Flock Safety, Inc. (or any ALPR vendor), including all exhibits, amendments, data retention schedules, and data-sharing provisions. 2. The authorizing ordinance, resolution, or official action approving the deployment of ALPR technology, including any public notice or hearing records. 3. Audit logs or access records showing which law enforcement agencies (local, state, federal, or out-of-state) have queried or accessed ALPR data collected within [CITY/TOWN NAME] jurisdiction. 4. Any correspondence between [CITY/TOWN NAME] officials and Flock Safety, Inc. regarding the deployment, renewal, data sharing, or data retention policies. 5. Budget documents, invoices, or grant applications related to ALPR procurement and operation, including any GIVE, COPS, or other federal/state grant funding used. I request that responsive records be provided in electronic format where available. If any records are withheld, I request a written explanation citing the specific statutory exemption relied upon. Please respond within the timeframe required by [YOUR STATE'S STATUTE]. If you have questions regarding this request, I can be reached at [YOUR EMAIL]. Thank you for your attention to this matter. [YOUR FULL NAME] [YOUR ADDRESS] [DATE]

Tip: Many states require a response within 5–10 business days. If you receive no response, that itself becomes evidence of non-compliance. Document every deadline and every silence. The paper trail is the weapon.

Tier 02 — Engaged Citizen — 1–2 Hours

Demand. Testify. Notify.

You filed the records request. Now put your name on it. A formal demand letter to every relevant official, public testimony at a council meeting, and a tip to local media. This is where one concerned resident becomes a problem they cannot ignore.

Step 4

Send the Demand Letter

One email. Every relevant official. CC your state legislator. You are a constituent exercising your First Amendment right to petition for redress of grievances. Include your name and address — you are not an anonymous complaint, you are a voter with standing. Set a 30-day response deadline. State that you will document both response and non-response publicly.

I have reviewed publicly available information about Flock Safety deployments nationally and have significant concerns about our city's exposure to legal liability, data security risks, and constitutional violations. Why CC your state legislator

This creates a legislative record. It puts the municipality on notice that the issue has been escalated beyond local channels. It also gives your state representative visibility into a constituent concern — several state-level ALPR privacy bills have originated from exactly this kind of constituent outreach.

Step 5

Testify at a City Council Meeting

Every municipality has a public comment period. Find your city's meeting schedule (usually on the city website or posted at city hall). Show up. Bring printed copies of your demand letter and the documented harms. Speak for three minutes. Be calm, specific, and on the record. This is your First Amendment right — speech and assembly — and it creates an official public record that cannot be deleted.

This data feeds a centralized database controlled by a private corporation in Atlanta, Georgia. I want to share three facts about this system that I believe the council and residents should be aware of. First — data sharing. The California Attorney General has sued El Cajon for illegal data sharing through Flock. Second — security. In January 2026, security researchers found 67 Flock cameras streaming to the internet without passwords. Live footage was viewable. A month of archives was accessible. A Texas sheriff queried the Flock database with the justification — and this is a direct quote from the audit log — "had an abortion, search for female." I will be documenting both your response and any non-response publicly. Thank you for your time.

A single-page static website documenting your city's Flock deployment. GitHub Pages is free. Include:

Step 10

Target the Leverage Points

Three moments of maximum leverage in any municipality:
  • Contract renewal date. Every Flock contract has one. City councils are most responsive to constituent pressure in the weeks before a renewal vote. Get this date from your FOIA request.
  • Budget season. When your city's annual budget is being drafted, Flock's line item is vulnerable. Show up to budget hearings.
  • Election cycles. City council and mayoral races create accountability pressure. Ask candidates their position on Flock. Challenge incumbents who authorized the deployment.

Tier 04 — Technical Contributor — Variable

Research. Build. Contribute.

For those with technical skills. FlockBlocker maintains an open-source adversarial research framework targeting the data layer of ALPR systems. This is published academic security research — the same discipline presented at NeurIPS, CVPR, USENIX Security, and ICML. No unauthorized access. No vehicle code violations. Legitimate adversarial ML research against deployed surveillance infrastructure.

The Core Thesis

Target the Data, Not the Camera

Most LPR countermeasures target optics — IR LEDs, reflective coatings, plate covers. These fail against modern camera tuning or create legal exposure. FlockBlocker targets the data layer: generating confident misreads that enter the database as ground truth and corrupt pattern-of-life analysis. A confident misread is more destructive to system integrity than a failed read, because failed reads get flagged while confident misreads propagate silently.

Research Vectors

Four Active Attack Surfaces

  • Character Confusion: Exploiting OCR ambiguity (0/O/D, 1/I/L, 8/B, 5/S). Visual modifications that cause the system to confidently read the wrong character.
  • Segmentation Boundary: Targeting character isolation — elements that cause the OCR pipeline to merge, split, or miscount characters in the plate string.
  • IR Phantom Injection: Pigments that appear identical to the human eye but diverge under 850nm/940nm IR illumination. The plate reads one thing to a person and something different to the camera.
  • EOT Adversarial Patch: Expectation Over Transformation — PyTorch-optimized adversarial patches with printability constraints, evaluated via composite metric across multiple OCR engines.
