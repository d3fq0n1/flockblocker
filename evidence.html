03 — Your Contradictions

Four Contradictions In The City's Own Letter

Below are four claims made in the April 15 response letter from City Administrator Haugh and Mayor Teske, paired with the City's own record. Every quotation is from the letter. Every rebuttal is sourced.

Contradiction 01 — The Wrong Document

"A quote, not the final agreement."

"The document previously sent to you was a quote, not the final agreement. We apologize for any confusion caused by providing the unexecuted quote. Please know that this oversight was unintentional."

The Chief of Police responded to a formal records demand on April 7, 2026 by attaching a document he represented as the Flock Safety contract. Contradiction 02 — "Not Infrastructure"

Network-connected surveillance hardware is "considered an operational investigative tool."

"The statute you cited pertains specifically to public infrastructure projects exceeding $25,000, which require a formal Request for Proposals (RFP) process. Not only is the Flock camera purchase not considered public infrastructure, it's considered an operational investigative tool."

Two network-connected, pole-mounted, always-on license plate readers — bolted to public structures, transmitting continuously to servers operated by a private corporation in Atlanta, integrated into a nationwide law enforcement data-sharing network — are infrastructure under any defensible definition. The City's position is that the classification hinges on the accounting label applied during procurement. Reclassifying surveillance hardware does not make it something other than surveillance hardware. The RFP question is preserved for discovery.

27 days of silence, one wrong document, one apology.

A formal decommission notice was served on March 19, 2026 to the Mayor, City Administrator, Chief of Police, and Sheriff — CC'd to a sitting state senator. The City's formal response arrived three days before the original 30-day deadline. A record this sparse does not support the claim that every resident's request is taken seriously. It supports the claim that this one had to be.

Contradiction 04 — "Threats And Intimidation"

"We will not make policy or safety decisions regarding our community based on threats or intimidation tactics."

Open Question 01

Was the November 2023 agreement the 8-camera, $50,200 deployment described in the quote?

Every question on this list will be the subject of a formal records request.
