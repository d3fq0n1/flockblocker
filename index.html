02 — The Approach

Target The Data Layer

Most LPR countermeasures attack the optical layer — IR flooding, reflective coatings, plate covers. These are cosmetic. They either fail against modern camera tuning or expose users to vehicle code violations.

FlockBlocker targets the database. Flock's commercial value is not any single plate read — it is the aggregated movement history. Pattern-of-life analysis requires data continuity. Corrupt the continuity and the downstream product degrades silently, invisibly, over time.

A failed read gets flagged or discarded — it is a visible gap in the record.
A confident misread enters the database as ground truth.

That distinction is the entire attack surface. The goal is not to defeat the camera. The goal is to make the data worthless to anyone trying to use it downstream — without the system ever knowing it happened.



Research directions: adversarial patch methodologies from published academic literature, OCR character ambiguity exploitation, and retroreflective interference designed to induce systematic misclassification. Distribution: open-source, organic, no coordination required.

03 — Origin

How This Started

On March 19, 2026, a formal decommission notice was sent to the Mayor, City Administrator, Police Chief, and Sheriff of Mauston, Wisconsin — CC'd to State Senator Patrick Testin.

The letter documented Flock Safety's data architecture, the ICE access vector, documented misuse cases nationally and in Wisconsin, and municipal liability exposure under the existing contract. Three specific demands were made: the authorizing ordinance, the full contract including data retention and third-party access terms, and the city's stated position on decommissioning.

A 30-day response window was established. No response becomes documented public record of officials ignoring a formal resident complaint with a sitting state senator CC'd.
