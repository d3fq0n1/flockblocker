01 — Legal & Regulatory

The Lawsuits Are Here

Class Action — Filed Feb 26, 2026

Gibbs Mura + Milberg v. Flock Safety — San Francisco Superior Court

SFPD's Flock cameras were searched by out-of-state agencies 1.6 million times in just 7 months. Los Altos: over 1 million times. The lawsuit alleges systematic violation of data sharing restrictions. Plaintiffs seek $2,500+ per violation. At 1.6 million violations, do the math.

State AG Action

California Attorney General Sues El Cajon

The California AG sued the City of El Cajon for Flock data sharing violations — sharing ALPR data without proper agreements, oversight, or resident notification. This is the state's top law enforcement officer saying Flock's data sharing model breaks the law.

Federal — U.S. Senate

Senator Wyden Calls for FTC Investigation

After 67 Flock cameras were found streaming live video without passwords (January 2026), Senator Ron Wyden formally called for an FTC investigation into Flock Safety's security practices. These are cameras deployed on public streets, capturing every passing vehicle, with zero authentication.

"Coordinated attack" ... "lawless" ... "terroristic"

04 — The Pattern

How Every Flock Contract Dies

Same sequence everywhere: a resident asks a question, the city ignores it, the resident documents the silence, the documentation reaches the council. The council realizes they signed a contract they didn't read for a system they don't control that feeds data to agencies they can't name. The contract dies.

Flock's business model depends on city councils not paying attention. The moment attention arrives, the contract becomes indefensible. 30+ cities walked away. Flock calls the attention "terroristic."
